Step-by-Step Guide

Your 9-module roadmap

01
Choosing a Profitable eCommerce Niche
Foundation +

The most important decision you'll make. Your niche determines your audience, your pricing power, and your long-term growth potential. Pick one with passion AND proven demand.

  • List your top 5 areas of genuine expertise or strong interest
  • Evaluate each niche for existing course demand (use Udemy, Skillshare, Kajabi marketplaces)
  • Check average price points — aim for niches where courses sell for $97–$997+
  • Identify a specific sub-niche to avoid competing with large players (e.g., "Dropshipping for single mums" vs "Dropshipping")
  • Validate that your target student has money and urgency to invest in learning
  • Shortlist 2–3 niche ideas before moving to validation
02
Researching Demand & Validating Ideas
Validation +

Don't build a course nobody wants. Validate before you invest weeks of creation time. Real validation means real people tell you they'd pay — not just "like" the idea.

  • Run keyword research using Google Trends, Ahrefs, or Ubersuggest for your topic
  • Browse Reddit, Facebook Groups, and Quora for recurring questions in your niche
  • Analyse top-selling courses on Udemy, Teachable, and Kajabi — read their reviews
  • Survey 10–20 ideal students via social media or email — ask what they'd pay
  • Run a "pre-sell" — offer the course before it's built and see if people buy
  • Set a validation threshold: 5+ pre-sales or 20+ email signups before building
03
Designing Your Course Curriculum
Design +

Great curriculum design is the difference between students who complete your course and rave about it versus those who ask for refunds. Structure for transformation, not just information.

  • Define the specific outcome students will achieve by the end of your course
  • Map the journey from Point A (student's current state) to Point B (desired outcome)
  • Break that journey into 5–10 logical modules, each building on the last
  • Within each module, create 3–7 lessons — keep each lesson under 10 minutes
  • Include assignments, worksheets, or action steps to reinforce learning
  • Plan a "quick win" in Module 1 to hook students early and reduce drop-offs
04
Creating Your Course Content
Production +

Production quality matters — but not as much as clarity and value. Start with minimal equipment and level up as revenue grows. Your knowledge is the product, not your camera.

  • Script or outline each lesson before recording — this cuts editing time by 60%
  • Start with a decent USB microphone and good room lighting (window light works)
  • Use Loom, Camtasia, or OBS for screen recording if teaching software/tools
  • Record in batches — commit 2–3 full days to record the entire course in one sprint
  • Use AI tools (Descript, Otter.ai) to auto-transcribe and clean up footage
  • Create workbooks, templates, and checklists as bonus resources
  • Add captions to all videos — improves accessibility and completion rates
05
Pricing & Positioning Your Course
Strategy +

Most creators undercharge dramatically. Price based on the value of the outcome — not the time it took to make. A $997 course that transforms a student's business is a bargain.

  • Research competitor pricing — aim to position in the mid-to-premium range
  • Price tiers: Mini-course $27–$97 | Signature course $297–$997 | Mastermind $1,000–$5,000+
  • Create 2–3 pricing tiers: Basic, Standard, VIP (with coaching or community access)
  • Consider payment plans — splitting $497 into 4x $147 dramatically increases conversions
  • Craft a compelling value stack: show the total value of all bonuses versus the price
  • Test your price with your warm audience first, then optimise based on data
06
Platforms to Host & Sell Your Course
Tech Setup +

Your platform is your storefront and classroom combined. Choose based on your tech comfort level, audience size, and whether you want an all-in-one solution or more control.

  • Kajabi — best all-in-one platform (email, landing pages, community, courses)
  • Teachable — beginner-friendly with solid sales tools and payment handling
  • Thinkific — great free tier; highly customisable; no transaction fees
  • Podia — perfect for digital downloads, memberships, and courses combined
  • Udemy / Skillshare — marketplace model; massive audience, but lower margins
  • Connect Stripe or PayPal for direct payments and set up automatic receipts
  • Set up a custom domain for credibility (e.g., learn.yourbrand.com)
07
Marketing Strategies That Drive Sales
Marketing +

Marketing is the engine of your course business. You need a system that attracts cold audiences, warms them up, and converts them into paying students — repeatedly and on autopilot.

  • Build your email list before launch — aim for 500+ engaged subscribers minimum
  • Create a lead magnet (free checklist, template, or mini-course) to grow your list fast
  • Choose 1–2 social platforms where your ideal student lives — go deep, not wide
  • Post educational content 4–5x per week that solves micro-problems from your course topic
  • Start a YouTube channel — long-form content is the best free lead generation engine
  • Run a 5-day email nurture sequence to new subscribers before pitching the course
  • Collaborate with complementary creators for joint webinars and cross-promotions
08
Launching Your Course
Launch +

A great launch creates urgency, builds social proof, and drives a sales surge. Follow a proven launch sequence rather than just "putting the course up and hoping."

  • Plan a 7–10 day open cart period with a hard-close deadline to create urgency
  • Start your pre-launch 3–4 weeks early with valuable content warming up your audience
  • Host a free live workshop or webinar 48 hours before launch — pitch at the end
  • Send a 5-email launch sequence: open → value → value → urgency → last chance
  • Offer a launch-week bonus (extra module, live Q&A, or 1:1 call) for early buyers
  • Share student testimonials and results in real time during the launch window
  • Close the cart with a strong final email — "last chance" emails drive 30–40% of sales
09
Scaling & Automating Your Sales
Scale +

Once you've validated your course, it's time to build an engine that runs without you. Evergreen funnels, paid ads, and automation turn your course into a 24/7 sales machine.

  • Build an evergreen webinar funnel using EverWebinar or WebinarJam on autopilot
  • Set up a Tripwire funnel: free lead magnet → $27 mini-course → main course upsell
  • Launch paid ads (Meta or YouTube) once you have a proven organic sales process
  • Recruit affiliates to promote your course for a 30–40% commission per sale
  • Create a student success system — happy students become your best marketers
  • Hire a VA to handle community management and customer support
  • Add a membership or mastermind tier for recurring revenue once core course is profitable

Monetisation Strategies

Four powerful ways to
generate course revenue

Smart creators combine multiple monetisation streams to maximise income and reduce reliance on a single source.

01

Direct Sales

Sell your course directly to your audience via your own website or course platform. You keep 90–100% of revenue, control pricing, and own the customer relationship entirely.

Best for: creators with an existing email list or social audience
02

Affiliate Partnerships

Recruit affiliates — other creators, influencers, or bloggers — to promote your course for a commission of 20–50%. This leverages other people's audiences to drive sales at zero upfront cost.

Best for: scaling beyond your own audience without ad spend
03

Funnels & Upsells

A well-designed funnel turns a $27 purchase into a $500+ customer journey. Order bumps, upsells, and downsells maximise your average order value from every buyer automatically.

Best for: maximising revenue per visitor with automation
04

Subscription Models

Convert one-time buyers into monthly members with a community, live calls, or fresh content drops. Memberships create predictable, recurring revenue that compounds over time.

Best for: established creators with an engaged community
